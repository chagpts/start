Ledger of Intelligence — A Film Script About Building an AI OCR Accounting System

This story follows three people trying to build a US accounting AI system. What begins as a simple idea about OCR and automation slowly turns into a much bigger realization: they are not building a feature, but a complete accounting platform.

Core Themes in This Script:

• LoRA and why US accounting-specific models are hard to find

• Why OCR alone cannot automate accounting correctly

• The difference between reading text and understanding accounting meaning

• Why AI must assist judgment, while engines enforce accounting rules

• Why the full system must be built as a pipeline

1 The Setup

A is frustrated because all the LoRA models they can find seem to come from India. B understands the technical and accounting challenges. C is new to development and keeps asking the questions that many beginners would ask.

This script is intentionally written so that even people with no development background can follow the conversation. Each technical term is explained inside the dialogue itself.

2 Main Idea of the Story

Concept Meaning in the Story
LoRA A lightweight way to adapt an AI model for a specialized task
OCR Technology that converts images or PDFs into readable text
Pipeline A step-by-step process where each stage handles one part of the work
Rule Engine A deterministic system that applies accounting logic without guessing
Closing Month-end or period-end accounting activities such as accruals and depreciation

3 Why This Matters

Many people assume that OCR plus AI is enough to automate accounting. But the same number can mean different things depending on the business context, the accounting standard, and the company policy.

In accounting, reading a document is not the same as understanding a transaction. That is why a real AI accounting system needs OCR, judgment, validation, posting logic, and closing logic.

Film Script

A:
I’m seriously stuck right now, like completely blocked and not making any progress at all.
I’ve been trying to build a US accounting AI system, but every LoRA model I find is from India.
I don’t even know if I’m looking in the wrong place or if this just doesn’t exist.
(나 지금 완전히 막혀서 아무 진전이 없어. 미국 회계 AI 만들려고 하는데 인도 LoRA밖에 안 보여. 내가 잘못 찾는 건지 아예 없는 건지 모르겠어.)

B:
What you’re seeing is actually normal, and it’s something a lot of people misunderstand at first.
It’s not that US LoRA models don’t exist, but they’re almost never publicly available due to licensing and data restrictions.
Especially in accounting, most valuable datasets are locked behind companies or legal protections.
(그거 사실 정상이고 많은 사람들이 처음에 오해하는 부분이다. 미국 LoRA가 없는 게 아니라 거의 공개가 안 되는 거야. 특히 회계 데이터는 기업이나 법적으로 막혀 있어서 더 그렇다.)

C:
Wait, before we go further, I need to understand something basic first.
What exactly is LoRA, and why does it matter so much for this kind of system?
I’ve never built anything like this before, so I’m trying to catch up.
(잠깐만, 나 기본부터 이해해야 할 것 같아. LoRA가 정확히 뭐고 왜 중요한 거야? 나 개발 처음이라 따라가는 중이야.)

B:
LoRA stands for Low-Rank Adaptation, and it’s basically a lightweight way to fine-tune AI models.
Instead of retraining a massive model from scratch, you just adjust small parts to specialize it.
Think of it like adding a focused skill set on top of an already smart system.
(LoRA는 기존 모델 전체를 다시 학습하는 게 아니라 일부만 조정하는 방식이다. 이미 똑똑한 AI에 특정 능력을 추가하는 거라고 보면 된다.)

A:
So if that’s the case, wouldn’t it still work if I just used an Indian LoRA as a starting point?
I mean, accounting is accounting, so maybe the differences aren’t that big.
Or am I completely underestimating how different these systems are?
(그럼 인도 LoRA라도 시작점으로 쓰면 안 돼? 회계는 비슷하지 않나? 아니면 내가 너무 쉽게 생각하는 건가?)

B:
You’re definitely underestimating it, and this is where most projects fail early on.
Indian models are typically based on IFRS and GST systems, while you need US GAAP.
Those differences affect everything from account structures to revenue recognition logic.
(그건 너무 쉽게 보는 거고 대부분 여기서 실패한다. 인도는 IFRS랑 GST 기반이고 너는 US GAAP이 필요하다. 계정 구조부터 수익 인식까지 다 다르다.)

C:
I’ve heard those terms before, but I don’t really understand them clearly.
Are IFRS and GAAP just different standards, or do they actually change how accounting works?
Like, would the same transaction be treated differently?
(그 용어 들어봤는데 잘 모르겠어. 그냥 기준만 다른 거야 아니면 처리 자체가 달라져? 같은 거래도 다르게 처리돼?)

B:
They absolutely can lead to different treatments for the same transaction, which is critical.
You can think of them as entirely different rulebooks that guide how financial data is recorded.
So using the wrong one will produce incorrect financial statements.
(같은 거래도 다르게 처리될 수 있어서 매우 중요하다. 완전히 다른 규칙이라고 보면 된다. 잘못 쓰면 재무제표가 틀린다.)

A:
Alright, so I accept that I need US-specific logic and data.
But that still leaves me wondering what the real core of this system should be.
I thought LoRA was the main challenge, but now I’m not so sure.
(그럼 미국 기준이 필요하다는 건 알겠어. 근데 진짜 핵심이 뭔지 모르겠다. LoRA가 제일 어려운 줄 알았는데 아닌 것 같아.)

B:
That’s exactly the shift you need to make in your thinking right now.
LoRA is just one small component, but the real challenge is designing the system itself.
You’re not building a model—you’re building an entire workflow.
(그게 지금 바뀌어야 하는 부분이다. LoRA는 작은 부분이고 진짜는 시스템 설계다. 모델이 아니라 전체 흐름을 만드는 거다.)

C:
When you say system design, I’m still not fully picturing it clearly.
Does that mean like how the screens look, or is it something deeper than that?
I feel like I’m missing the big picture here.
(시스템 설계라고 하면 아직 감이 안 와. 화면 디자인 같은 거야 아니면 더 깊은 거야? 전체 그림이 안 잡혀.)

B:
It’s much deeper than UI or screens, and it’s more about how data flows and gets processed.
System design means defining how each component interacts and passes information forward.
In your case, it’s a chain of transformations from raw documents to financial records.
(UI보다 훨씬 깊고 데이터 흐름 설계다. 각 단계가 어떻게 연결되는지 정하는 거다. 문서에서 재무데이터로 가는 과정 전체다.)

A:
Okay, let me explain what I had in mind so far.
I imagined something simple where OCR reads documents and then AI generates journal entries.
I thought everything could be handled in one intelligent step.
(내 생각은 단순했어. OCR이 문서 읽고 AI가 분개 만드는 거. 한 번에 다 되는 줄 알았어.)

B:
That sounds simple on the surface, but in reality, it’s far more layered than that.
What you’re describing is actually a pipeline, not a single step.
Each stage has to do a specific job before passing data to the next.
(겉보기엔 단순하지만 실제론 훨씬 복잡하다. 그건 하나가 아니라 파이프라인이다. 단계별로 처리해야 한다.)

C:
I’ve heard the word pipeline before, but I don’t fully understand it.
Is it like a sequence of steps where each part does one thing?
Almost like an assembly line in a factory?
(파이프라인 들어봤는데 잘 모르겠어. 단계별로 처리하는 거야? 공장 라인 같은 거야?)

B:
That’s actually a perfect way to think about it, and it’s a very useful analogy.
Each stage transforms the data slightly and prepares it for the next stage.
If one stage fails or is wrong, everything after it becomes unreliable.
(그 비유가 정확하다. 각 단계가 데이터를 조금씩 바꾸고 다음 단계로 넘긴다. 하나라도 틀리면 뒤에 다 틀린다.)

A:
So if I understand this correctly, I shouldn’t try to solve everything with AI alone.
Instead, I need to split the problem into structured steps and control each part carefully.
That already feels very different from how I was thinking before.
(그럼 AI 하나로 다 해결하려고 하면 안 되는 거네. 단계별로 나눠야 하고. 완전히 생각이 바뀌네.)

B:
Exactly, and this is the most important realization you can have at this stage.
AI is just one component, and in accounting it should mostly assist decisions.
The actual execution must be handled by deterministic systems.
(맞다. 이게 제일 중요한 포인트다. AI는 보조 역할이고 실제 실행은 코드 기반 시스템이 해야 한다.)

C:
When you say deterministic, I want to make sure I understand that term properly.
Does it mean the system always produces the same result given the same input?
So there’s no randomness or guessing involved?
(deterministic이라는 게 뭔지 알고 싶어. 같은 입력이면 항상 같은 결과 나오는 거야? 랜덤이 없는 거야?)

B:
That’s exactly what it means, and it’s critical for accounting systems.
You cannot afford uncertainty when dealing with financial data.
Every number must be reproducible and explainable.
(맞다. 같은 입력이면 항상 같은 결과다. 회계는 불확실성이 있으면 안 된다. 모든 숫자는 재현 가능해야 한다.)

A:
I think I finally understand the scale of what I’m trying to build.
This isn’t just about connecting OCR to an AI model and calling it done.
It’s about building a complete system that handles interpretation, validation, and execution.
(이제 내가 만드는 게 뭔지 알겠다. OCR이랑 AI 붙이는 게 아니라 해석, 검증, 실행까지 다 하는 시스템이다.)

C:
And even as someone new to development, I can see how each piece matters now.
It’s not about one powerful tool, but how everything works together step by step.
That actually makes it feel more understandable, not less.
(개발 몰라도 이제 이해된다. 하나의 강한 도구가 아니라 연결이 중요하다. 오히려 더 명확해졌다.)

B:
That’s the mindset you need if you want this to actually work in the real world.
Let me summarize it in one sentence so you never forget it.
AI handles judgment, engines enforce rules, and the entire system operates as a pipeline.
(이게 실제로 만들기 위한 사고방식이다. 한 줄로 정리해주면 AI는 판단, 엔진은 실행, 전체는 파이프라인이다.)

A:
Then we’re not just building a feature anymore.
We’re building an intelligent accounting system from the ground up.
And now, finally, I know where to start.
(이제 기능이 아니라 시스템이다. 처음부터 다시 만드는 거다. 이제 어디서 시작할지 알겠다.)
