ITAC Interface Testing Report (Non-Reliance)

This report documents an end-to-end audit workpaper for an automated interface: Shopify / Stripe → ChaGPT App → QuickBooks. The audit approach is Non-Reliance (i.e., no reliance on ChaGPT App controls / no SOC reliance), and focuses on direct source-to-GL validation.

ITAC Non-Reliance Interface Completeness Interface Accuracy Duplicate & Cutoff Control / Significant Deficiency Scenario
Scope Summary:
We tested whether transactions originating from Shopify and Stripe are complete, accurate, non-duplicative, and appropriately classified when recorded in QuickBooks via the ChaGPT App integration. This report includes sampling methodology, 10 sample tie-outs, exception analysis, expanded testing approach, and severity assessment concluding in a Significant Deficiency scenario (with notes on Control Deficiency alternative).

1. Background & System Description

Management uses the ChaGPT App (a custom integration application) to transmit sales, refunds, fees, and payouts from Shopify and Stripe to QuickBooks Online. The interface is system-driven (API/webhook-based), and transactions are posted to the general ledger through system-generated entries (e.g., sales receipts, deposits, expenses, refund receipts).

  • Upstream: Shopify (orders/refunds/taxes/shipping) and Stripe (charges/fees/payouts)
  • Middleware: ChaGPT App (mapping, transformation, sync scheduling, error queue, retry logic)
  • Downstream: QuickBooks (GL posting, bank feeds, reconciliation)

2. Audit Strategy (Non-Reliance)

We did not place reliance on ChaGPT App’s controls. Accordingly, we performed direct testing of completeness and accuracy by reconciling source transactions to amounts recorded in QuickBooks.

Non-Reliance Implication:
Assurance is obtained primarily through source-to-QuickBooks tie-outs, targeted reconciliations, and expanded testing where deviations are identified.

3. Control Objective

To determine whether transactions originating in Shopify and Stripe are:

  • Complete (all relevant transactions recorded in QBO)
  • Accurate (gross, fee, net, and attributes match source)
  • Non-duplicative (unique IDs not posted twice)
  • Properly classified (appropriate GL accounts)
  • Properly cut off (recorded in the appropriate reporting period)

4. Population

Population obtained from management for the audit period:

  • Period: [Jan 1 – Dec 31, 20XX]
  • Total transactions: [18,742]
  • Population sources: Shopify exports; Stripe exports; QBO transaction exports; ChaGPT App sync logs

5. Sampling Methodology

5.1 Non-Statistical (Judgmental) Sampling Rationale

We selected a judgmental sample of 10 items focusing on higher-risk characteristics:

  • High-dollar sales
  • Period-end activity (cutoff risk)
  • Refunds / returns
  • Items with sync retries / warnings in the app log

5.2 Selection Documentation

Selection Type Criteria Planned Count
High Value Top amounts by gross sales 4
Period End Within ±5 days of month/quarter-end 3
Refunds Refund transactions / negative gross 2
Exceptions App log shows retry/warning 1

6. Test Procedures Performed

  • Agreed unique identifier (Shopify Order ID / Stripe Charge ID) to QuickBooks reference
  • Traced Gross, Fee, and Net from source → QBO
  • Inspected posting date for cutoff
  • Verified GL account classification (Sales, Merchant Fees, Clearing, Refunds, Sales Tax)
  • Inspected QBO transaction list for duplicates for the same unique identifier

8. Exception Summary

  • Accuracy deviation: Fee mismatch (minor net impact) for one sales transaction.
  • Classification deviation: Shipping portion mapped to an incorrect income account.
Interpretation:
The exceptions appear limited in scope and are not indicative of pervasive completeness or duplication failures. However, classification issues may affect reporting (e.g., gross margin, shipping KPI) and warrant remediation.

9. Root Cause Discussion (Inquiry with Management)

  • Fee mismatch tied to app rounding logic for certain discount/tax combinations.
  • Shipping mapping error caused by an outdated mapping rule in ChaGPT App configuration.
  • Management reviews monthly revenue summaries and performs bank/payout reconciliation; exceptions were not previously escalated.

10. Expanded Testing Method (Fail Response)

Because exceptions were identified, we performed targeted expanded testing to determine whether issues were isolated or repeatable:

  • Attribute-based expansion: all transactions with shipping lines during [month] (classification risk).
  • Targeted fee expansion: additional transactions with discount/tax combinations that trigger rounding differences.

Expanded testing is designed to evaluate whether the exception is systematic (configuration/logic) vs isolated. If expansion finds no additional issues, this supports an isolated deficiency conclusion.

11. Compensating Controls Considered

  • Monthly management review of sales summaries (Shopify vs QBO totals)
  • Stripe payout-to-bank reconciliation (deposit matching)
  • Periodic review of integration settings
Compensating control assessment:
Detective controls exist and are expected to identify gross-level completeness issues. However, they may not detect classification errors unless the review includes account-level analytics.

12. Likelihood & Impact Assessment (Severity Framework)

12.1 Likelihood

  • Assessment: Moderate
  • Rationale: Two exceptions observed; one suggests a configuration rule that could affect a subset of transactions.

12.2 Impact

  • Assessment: Low to Moderate
  • Rationale: Exceptions do not indicate missing/duplicate revenue at scale. Primary impact is account classification and minor fee variance.

13. Severity Determination

Severity Level When It Fits Why It Could Apply Here
Control Deficiency Low likelihood and/or low impact; isolated or easily detected If expanded testing finds no additional exceptions and the issue is limited to one-off configuration drift
Significant Deficiency Warrants governance attention; meaningful reporting risk If classification errors affect a meaningful portion of shipping revenue or fee logic impacts a large subset across periods
Severity Conclusion (Scenario):
Based on initial exceptions, and assuming expanded testing identifies additional classification issues across a subset of shipping transactions without affecting overall revenue completeness, this scenario is evaluated as a Significant Deficiency (financial reporting presentation risk and recurring configuration issue).

Alternative conclusion: If expanded testing indicates the issue is isolated and promptly corrected, the severity would be a Control Deficiency.

14. Overall Conclusion

The ChaGPT App interface generally records transactions from Shopify and Stripe into QuickBooks; however, exceptions were identified related to fee accuracy (minor) and account classification (shipping mapping). Given the potential for recurring impact within a subset and limited detective controls at the account level, this scenario is assessed as a Significant Deficiency (non-material but governance-relevant).

15. Recommendations

  • Implement change management and approvals for ChaGPT App mapping rules (especially shipping/tax treatment).
  • Enhance monitoring: weekly exception report from the app log and documented review.
  • Add account-level analytics in monthly review to detect classification shifts.
  • Standardize rounding policy for fee calculations and validate against Stripe reports.
  • Restrict access to integration configuration; perform quarterly access reviews.

Auditor Finding
We identified fee variance and recurring shipping mapping risk. While completeness appears intact, classification accuracy is not fully controlled and could affect reporting and KPIs.

Management Response
We will update mapping rules, implement weekly log review, and add account-level analytics to our monthly close.

Auditor Conclusion
Based on likelihood and impact, and assuming expanded testing supports recurrence within a subset, we conclude a significant deficiency for the interface process in this scenario.
