Accounting Automation in Practice — How Data Flows Into the GL and Updates Financial Statements

In most modern setups, nobody is “typing numbers into financial statements.” Instead, the financial statements are the output of a system that is continuously posting to the general ledger (GL).

So the real story is:

Source systems → standardized data intake (표준화된 데이터 유입/수집) → coding/posting rules → GL posting → subledger rollups → financial statements update automatically.

The finance team’s work shifts from data entry to controls, review, and exception handling.

1 What people mean by “accounting automation” now

Accounting automation today is mostly about building a reliable pipeline (업무/데이터 처리 흐름) where operational data (bank activity, invoices, card spend, billing events) is captured in a standardized format and posted into the GL with consistent logic.

When implemented well, the GL becomes the single source of truth, and financial statements become a real-time report layer.

Training Dialogue (Senior → Junior)

Senior: When people say “accounting automation” now, they don’t mean humans disappear. They mean we build a pipeline(파이프라인: 데이터가 흘러가는 자동 처리 흐름) that posts operational data into the GL reliably.

Junior: So financial statements aren’t “written” automatically—they’re just reports generated from whatever is in the GL?

Senior: Exactly. The hard part is standardized intake(표준화 인입: 같은 포맷으로 들어오게 만들기) and consistent posting rules. If those are weak, you just automate mistakes.

Junior: What’s the first thing you check when you design the pipeline?

Senior: The source systems and the data shape—bank, AP, cards, billing—and then the COA mapping and exception workflow.

Korean Translation

선임: 요즘 “회계 자동화”라고 하면 사람이 사라진다는 뜻이 아니라, 운영 데이터가 GL로 안정적으로 들어가도록 파이프라인(데이터 자동 처리 흐름)을 만든다는 의미야.

후임: 그럼 재무제표는 자동으로 “작성”되는 게 아니라, GL에 들어있는 데이터를 기반으로 리포트가 나오는 거네요?

선임: 맞아. 핵심은 표준화 인입(같은 형식으로 유입)과 일관된 전표 규칙이야. 이게 약하면 실수를 자동화하는 꼴이 돼.

후임: 파이프라인 설계할 때 제일 먼저 확인하는 건 뭐예요?

선임: 은행, AP, 카드, 빌링 같은 소스 시스템과 데이터 형태부터 보고, 그 다음 COA 매핑과 예외 처리 흐름을 잡아.

2 Bank feeds → auto-categorization → auto journal entries

What’s automated

  • Daily bank feeds (ACH(ACH: 미국식 계좌이체/자동이체 네트워크), wires, deposits, withdrawals) flow into the accounting system.
  • Transactions are auto-matched to known vendors/customers, historical patterns, or memo descriptors.
  • The system proposes (or auto-posts) entries like:
  • Bank fees → Bank charges expense
  • Merchant deposits → AR clearing / Sales clearing (sales clearing: 매출 임시계정/클리어링 계정)
  • Rent → Rent expense
  • Debt service → Interest expense + loan payable

Why this matters

It eliminates the old workflow:

> download bank CSV → clean it → manually enter cash activity → hope it ties out

What accountants still do

  • Review exceptions (new vendors, ambiguous memos)
  • Confirm correct classification (OPEX vs CAPEX(운영비 vs 자본적지출), COGS vs operating(매출원가 vs 판관비/운영비))
  • Ensure period cutoffs and accrual logic are right
  • Approve posting thresholds (e.g., auto-post under $X)

Training Dialogue (Senior → Junior)

Senior: Bank feeds can auto-post, but our job is managing exceptions: new vendors, unclear memos, unusual patterns.

Junior: If the bank balance reconciles, is that enough to trust the automation?

Senior: Not really. Reconciliation proves totals, not correctness of classification. Merchant deposits usually shouldn’t become revenue directly—often they go to an AR or sales clearing(임시로 모아두는 계정) account first.

Junior: So we shouldn’t set everything to auto-post immediately?

Senior: Right. Use thresholds: auto-post under a certain amount, propose-only above it, and review sensitive accounts.

Junior: How do cutoffs show up in bank automation?

Senior: Cash timing and accrual timing differ. You still need accrual entries when the bank date doesn’t reflect the economic period.

Korean Translation

선임: 은행 피드는 자동 기장도 되지만, 우리는 예외(신규 거래처/애매한 메모/특이 패턴)를 관리해야 해.

후임: 은행 잔액이 리콘되면 자동화를 믿어도 되나요?

선임: 꼭 그렇진 않아. 리콘은 합계가 맞는지 보는 거지 분류가 맞는지까지 보장하진 않아. 가맹점 입금은 보통 매출로 바로 잡지 않고 세일즈 클리어링(매출 임시계정)을 먼저 타는 경우가 많아.

후임: 그럼 처음부터 전부 자동포스팅으로 두면 안 되겠네요?

선임: 맞아. 금액 기준으로 자동포스팅/제안(propose-only)을 나누고, 민감 계정은 별도로 리뷰해야 해.

후임: 월말 컷오프는 은행 자동화에서 어떻게 보나요?

선임: 현금 기준과 발생주의 기준이 달라. 은행일자가 경제적 기간을 반영하지 않으면 발생주의 조정이 필요해.

3 AP automation: invoice capture + 2/3-way match + posting

What’s automated

Invoices enter the system via:

  • Vendor portals
  • Email inboxes
  • EDI (larger vendors)(EDI: 기업 간 전자문서/데이터 교환)
  • Scans/PDFs with OCR

The system extracts fields (vendor, invoice #, date, amounts, line items), then:

  • Checks duplicates
  • Validates tax and totals
  • Routes for approval (workflow)
  • Runs 2-way match (PO vs invoice)(발주서-인보이스 대조) or 3-way match (PO vs invoice vs receiving)(발주서-인보이스-검수/입고 대조)
  • Posts to AP subledger and GL

Where “auto-posting” becomes real

Once the invoice is approved and matched:

  • Expense account and department can be pulled from:(계정/부서 자동 지정 근거)
  • PO coding(PO 코딩: 발주 단계에서 계정/부서 태깅)
  • Vendor rules (“Adobe invoices → software subscriptions”)(벤더 규칙: 거래처별 자동 분류)
  • Item master mapping (“MRO supplies → maintenance expense”)(품목 마스터 매핑: 품목→계정 연결)
  • Accrual timing can be automated (service period recognition, prepaid logic)

What accountants still do

  • Handle non-PO invoices and messy vendors
  • Review unusual coding (repairs vs capital, marketing vs customer acquisition)
  • Resolve price/quantity variances, partial receipts, credit memos
  • Ensure sales tax/use tax treatment is correct

Training Dialogue (Senior → Junior)

Senior: In AP, the real win isn’t OCR—it’s matching. Controls come from 2-way and 3-way match.

Junior: What’s the biggest practical difference between 2-way and 3-way?

Senior: 3-way adds receiving(입고/검수). If physical goods are involved, 3-way is much safer than paying based on an invoice alone.

Junior: After match and approval, does coding happen automatically?

Senior: If PO coding is clean, yes. But for non-PO invoices, you’ll rely on vendor rules and item mappings—and still review judgment calls like repair vs capitalization.

Junior: Duplicate invoices—can we trust the system to catch them?

Senior: It catches many, but not all. That’s why we do targeted reviews on high-risk vendors and recurring spend categories.

Korean Translation

선임: AP에서 진짜 성과는 OCR이 아니라 매칭이야. 2-way/3-way 매칭이 통제를 만들어.

후임: 2-way랑 3-way의 실무 차이가 가장 큰 게 뭐예요?

선임: 3-way는 입고/검수가 추가돼. 실물이 있는 구매는 인보이스만 보고 지급하는 것보다 훨씬 안전해.

후임: 매칭과 승인까지 끝나면 코딩은 자동으로 되나요?

선임: PO 코딩이 깨끗하면 거의 자동이야. 하지만 non-PO는 벤더 규칙/품목 매핑을 쓰되 판단(수선 vs 자본화)은 여전히 리뷰해야 해.

후임: 중복 인보이스는 시스템이 다 잡아주나요?

선임: 많이 잡지만 전부는 아니야. 그래서 고위험 벤더나 반복비용은 타깃 리뷰를 해.

4 Corporate cards + employee expenses → automated coding & policy checks

What’s automated

  • Card transactions feed in daily.
  • Employees attach receipts (mobile capture).
  • System applies:
  • Policy rules (limits, categories)
  • Merchant-based coding
  • Auto-splitting for tips, taxes, etc.
  • Approved expenses post into GL (often through an expense subledger).

What accountants still do

  • Exceptions (missing receipts, out-of-policy spend)
  • Reclassifications (client meals vs travel vs office)
  • Proper cost center allocation

Training Dialogue (Senior → Junior)

Senior: For corporate cards, automation is nice—but the bigger value is catching policy violations(정책 위반) early.

Junior: What violations happen most often?

Senior: Missing receipts, out-of-policy categories, or spending above limits. The system flags them; we confirm justification and evidence.

Junior: Does merchant-based coding ever misclassify?

Senior: All the time. That’s why we require cost center/project tags(코스트센터/프로젝트 태그) and review the largest items monthly.

Junior: Can we trust automatic splitting for tips and taxes?

Senior: Only after validation, especially during rollout(도입 초기/전사 적용 단계).

Korean Translation

선임: 법인카드는 자동화도 좋지만, 더 큰 가치는 정책 위반을 빨리 잡는 데 있어.

후임: 어떤 위반이 제일 자주 나와요?

선임: 영수증 누락, 정책 외 카테고리, 한도 초과 같은 것들. 시스템이 플래그를 세우면 사유와 증빙을 확인해.

후임: 가맹점 기반 코딩이 틀릴 때도 있나요?

선임: 자주 있어. 그래서 코스트센터/프로젝트 태그를 필수로 받고 큰 금액은 월 단위로 리뷰해.

후임: 팁/세금 자동 분할은 믿어도 돼요?

선임: 도입(롤아웃) 초기에는 반드시 검증하고 나서 적용해.

5 AR automation: invoicing + cash application + revenue logic (sometimes)

What’s automated

  • Invoices generated from CRM/billing systems (CRM/빌링 시스템: 고객/청구 관리 시스템) (Stripe, Zuora, Salesforce billing, etc.)
  • Customer payments feed in (ACH/card).
  • Cash application uses:
  • Remittance data
  • Invoice # matching
  • Customer rules

Some companies also automate parts of revenue recognition:

  • Contract data → schedules
  • Deferred revenue rollforwards
  • Allocation rules (ASC 606) in specialized systems

What accountants still do

  • Unapplied cash and short pay(일부만 지급/미달 지급)
  • Dispute handling and credit memos
  • Revenue judgments—performance obligations, variable consideration, cutoffs (판단: 수행의무/변동대가/기간 귀속)

Training Dialogue (Senior → Junior)

Senior: In AR, the automation payoff is biggest in cash application. Unapplied cash destroys reporting.

Junior: Isn’t it just matching by invoice number?

Senior: In practice, customers often pay multiple invoices together, and remittance data is messy.

Junior: How do we handle short pays—can that be automated?

Senior: The system can post a difference, but it can’t decide if it’s a discount, a dispute, or an error. That judgment drives credit memos and revenue adjustments.

Junior: What about automating revenue recognition with ASC 606 tools?

Senior: Schedules can be automated, but judgments—performance obligations, variable consideration, and cutoffs(핵심 판단 3가지)—still require human review and strong documentation.

Korean Translation

선임: AR에서 자동화 효과는 현금 적용에서 가장 커. 미적용 현금이 쌓이면 리포팅이 망가져.

후임: 인보이스 번호로 매칭하면 끝 아닌가요?

선임: 실제로는 여러 인보이스 합산 지급도 많고, 리미턴스 데이터가 지저분해.

후임: short pay(일부만 지급)는 자동으로 처리할 수 있나요?

선임: 차액을 잡아줄 수는 있어도, 할인/분쟁/오류 중 무엇인지는 판단 못 해. 그 판단이 크레딧 메모와 매출 조정으로 이어져.

후임: ASC 606 툴로 매출인식을 자동화하면요?

선임: 스케줄 계산은 자동화돼도, 수행의무/변동대가/컷오프 같은 판단은 사람 리뷰와 문서화가 필요해.

6 “Auto financial statements” is really “auto rollups from a clean GL”

> The Balance Sheet and Income Statement update automatically because they’re reports on the GL —
> not because the company is “writing” the financial statements automatically.

As transactions post into the GL (directly or via subledgers), reporting layers calculate:

  • Trial balance
  • Department/entity rollups
  • FX translation (if multi-currency)
  • Consolidation eliminations (if multi-entity)

Training Dialogue (Senior → Junior)

Senior: “Auto financial statements” really means the GL is clean, so reporting can roll up automatically.

Junior: What does “clean GL” mean in practice?

Senior: Consistent coding and subledger tie-outs. If the foundation is messy, the report will be messy.

Junior: If a report looks wrong, should we fix the report mapping first?

Senior: Usually no. Trace the source transaction and mapping rules first, then adjust report lines last.

Korean Translation

선임: “재무제표 자동”은 GL가 깨끗해서 리포트가 자동 롤업된다는 뜻이야.

후임: 실무에서 GL가 깨끗하다는 건 뭐예요?

선임: 코딩이 일관되고 서브레저 대사가 잘 되는 것. 기반이 지저분하면 리포트도 지저분해.

후임: 리포트가 이상하면 리포트 매핑부터 고치면 되나요?

선임: 보통은 아니야. 원천 거래와 매핑 룰부터 추적하고 마지막에 리포트 라인을 조정해.

7 Close automation: reconciliations, flux analysis, and continuous accounting

This is where US accountants often feel the biggest shift.

What’s automated now

  • Bank rec auto-match (transactions ↔ book entries)
  • Intercompany matching
  • Auto-generated accrual suggestions (based on prior months or contracts)
  • Close checklists and workflow tracking
  • Flux analysis flags (변동 분석 경고/플래그) (material movements vs thresholds)
  • Journal entry controls (approval routing, evidence links)

In strong implementations, the “month-end close” becomes less of a cliff and more like: continuous posting + daily/weekly review + month-end tie-out and judgment calls.

Training Dialogue (Senior → Junior)

Senior: Close automation isn’t about cramming work into month-end faster. It’s about shifting to continuous accounting.

Junior: When flux analysis flags something, what’s the right response?

Senior: You explain the “why.” Drill down to drivers and attach support. The tool flags; we interpret.

Junior: Can we rely on auto accrual suggestions?

Senior: Not at first. Validate samples, keep thresholds conservative, then expand.

Korean Translation

선임: 마감 자동화는 월말에 더 빨리 몰아치는 게 아니라 상시 회계로 바꾸는 거야.

후임: 변동분석 플래그가 뜨면 어떻게 대응하죠?

선임: “왜 변했는지”를 설명해야 해. 원인까지 드릴다운하고 근거를 붙여. 툴은 표시만 하고 해석은 우리가 해.

후임: 자동 발생 제안은 믿고 써도 돼요?

선임: 초반엔 샘플 검증부터 하고, 임계값은 보수적으로 두고, 점진적으로 확장해.

8 What makes or breaks automation: rules + master data + controls

Automation only feels “real” when these are in place:

  • Vendor/customer master data is clean
  • A consistent chart of accounts and mapping logic exists
  • Policies exist for:
  • Auto-post vs propose-only
  • Materiality thresholds
  • Exception workflows
  • Strong audit trail:
  • Source document attached
  • Approval history visible
  • Change logs for edits and overrides

For SOX environments, this is crucial: automation increases speed, but it also increases the need for system controls and monitoring.

Training Dialogue (Senior → Junior)

Senior: Automation succeeds or fails on rules, master data, and controls—not on fancy features.

Junior: How do we decide propose-only versus auto-post?

Senior: By amount, account sensitivity, vendor trust, and historical error rates. Start cautious, then scale.

Korean Translation

선임: 자동화의 성패는 룰/마스터데이터/통제에 달려 있어. 기능이 화려하다고 성공하는 게 아니야.

후임: propose-only랑 auto-post는 어떻게 결정해요?

선임: 금액, 계정 민감도, 벤더 신뢰도, 과거 오류율로. 처음엔 보수적으로 시작해서 안정화되면 확장해.

9 The honest boundary: automation doesn’t replace judgment

It replaces:

  • Copying numbers
  • Repetitive postings
  • Matching and routing

It does not replace:

  • Classification judgment (OPEX vs CAPEX(운영비 vs 자본적지출), COGS vs operating(매출원가 vs 운영비), below-the-line items)
  • Estimates (accruals, reserves, impairment)
  • Cutoffs and revenue judgment
  • Unusual transactions (M&A, restructurings, complex leases, equity)

So the job shifts to: review, exception management, governance, and analysis.
