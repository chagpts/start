Subpart F, GILTI, and Korean CFC — International Tax Study Case with Beginner-Friendly Explanation

This page is designed for learners who want to understand how the United States and Korea deal with foreign profits that remain inside foreign subsidiaries. The three key topics are Subpart F, GILTI, and the Korean CFC rule.

The goal is twofold: first, to practice business English and accounting English; second, to understand a core international tax idea: a government may tax certain foreign earnings even if no actual dividend is paid.

Study Goal

Learn the policy purpose, basic mechanics, and practical language used to explain anti-deferral rules in the United States and Korea. This page is written as a study guide, not as legal advice.

Beginner Starting Point

If you are completely new to tax, begin with these three ideas:

  • A company may earn profit.
  • A shareholder usually expects tax when that profit is paid out as a dividend.
  • But anti-deferral rules may say: “We will tax some of that income now, even if the cash has not been distributed yet.”

That third idea is the heart of Subpart F, GILTI, and the Korean CFC rule.

1 Why Do These Rules Exist?

Suppose a taxpayer forms or owns a company in a foreign country. Now suppose that foreign company earns income and does not pay a dividend. Many people would naturally think, “If the cash stays in the foreign company, there should be no shareholder-level tax yet.”

Governments worry that this can be used to delay tax indefinitely, especially when the foreign company is located in a low-tax jurisdiction. A taxpayer might move profits offshore, leave them there, and argue that tax can be postponed for many years simply because no dividend was declared. That strategy is commonly described as tax deferral.

Simple explanation of deferral

Deferral means delaying tax to a later time. Anti-deferral rules do not say that every foreign company is a problem. They say that some foreign income should not escape current taxation merely because it is kept inside a foreign corporation.

4 Important Terminology: “Deemed” vs “Constructive”

Which expression is better for the Korean CFC rule?

For the Korean CFC rule, the better and more standard expression is deemed dividend, not constructive dividend.

Why? Because the Korean CFC rule says that undistributed earnings of a foreign corporation may be treated by law as if they were distributed. That is exactly the idea captured by the word deemed.

By contrast, constructive dividend is a different tax concept more commonly used in situations where a corporation gives an economic benefit to a shareholder without formally declaring a dividend, such as paying the shareholder’s personal expenses or allowing personal use of corporate property.

Practical conclusion

When explaining Korean CFC rules in English, use deemed dividend or deemed distributed income. That is the more accurate international tax terminology.

5 Technical Rule: Korean CFC Rule

Controlled foreign corporations (CFCs)
Now let us move from the simple explanation to the technical rule. This is the part that should appear in a more professional study page.

Korean CFC Rule — Technical Description

Under the Korean CFC rule, when a Korean national or company directly or indirectly owns at least 10% in a foreign corporation and the foreign corporation’s average effective income tax rate for the three most recent consecutive years is 16.8% or less, the undistributed earnings of the foreign corporation (CFC) shall be deemed to be paid as a dividend to the Korean national or company, thereby becoming subject to tax in Korea.

In simple terms, if a Korean taxpayer has a significant ownership interest in a low-tax foreign company, Korea may tax the foreign company’s undistributed earnings as though they had already been distributed.

Beginner Translation of the Rule

  • If a Korean taxpayer owns a meaningful stake in a foreign company,
  • and that foreign company is taxed very lightly,
  • and the profit is left inside the company instead of being paid out,
  • Korea may still tax that profit as if the shareholder had already received a dividend.

This is why the phrase deemed dividend is important. It does not mean actual cash was paid. It means the law treats the income as if it had been paid.

6 Client Asks the Basic Question

Scene 1

Client: I often hear people talk about Subpart F and GILTI in U.S. tax discussions. Does Korea have something similar?

Accountant: Yes, Korea has a similar anti-deferral concept, although the structure is not identical. In the United States, the two major concepts are Subpart F and GILTI. In Korea, the closest concept is the CFC rule.

Client: So the names are different, but the purpose is similar?

Accountant: Exactly. Both countries are trying to prevent taxpayers from moving profit to low-tax foreign entities and delaying tax simply by keeping that profit offshore.

Client: So this is really about timing of taxation?

Accountant: That is a very good way to put it. The key question is not only, “Was there an actual cash dividend?” The key question is also, “Should the law require current taxation now, even though the money is still inside the foreign company?”

Korean Translation

고객: 미국 세법 이야기할 때 Subpart F와 GILTI라는 말을 자주 듣는데요. 한국에도 비슷한 제도가 있나요?

회계사: 네, 한국에도 비슷한 과세이연 방지 개념이 있습니다. 다만 구조가 완전히 같지는 않습니다. 미국에서는 대표적으로 Subpart F와 GILTI가 있고, 한국에서는 가장 가까운 개념이 CFC 규정입니다.

고객: 그럼 이름은 다르지만 목적은 비슷하다는 말이군요?

회계사: 맞습니다. 두 나라 모두 이익을 저세율 해외법인으로 옮기고, 그 이익을 계속 해외에 남겨 둠으로써 과세를 미루는 구조를 막으려는 목적이 있습니다.

고객: 결국 과세 시점의 문제라고 볼 수 있겠네요?

회계사: 아주 좋은 표현입니다. 핵심 질문은 단지 “실제 현금 배당이 있었는가?”가 아닙니다. “그 돈이 외국회사 안에 남아 있어도 지금 과세해야 하는가?” 까지 함께 보는 것입니다.

7 What Does “Taxed Without a Dividend” Mean?

Scene 2

Client: I still do not fully understand the phrase “taxed without a dividend.” How can tax arise if I did not actually receive money?

Accountant: Under normal thinking, you would expect tax when the company pays you a dividend. That is why this area feels strange at first. But anti-deferral rules can override that timing result.

Client: So the law can ignore the fact that no cash was paid?

Accountant: In a limited sense, yes. The law may say: “We know the money remains in the foreign corporation, but because of the ownership structure and low-tax environment, we will treat part of that income as currently taxable to the shareholder.”

Client: So it is not saying I physically got paid. It is saying the tax law wants to accelerate the timing.

Accountant: Exactly. That is why the words deemed dividend are so important. The law is creating a tax result even though there was no actual dividend payment.

Korean Translation

고객: 그래도 “배당 없이 과세된다”는 말이 완전히 이해되지는 않습니다. 실제로 돈을 받지 않았는데 어떻게 세금이 생기나요?

회계사: 일반적으로는 회사가 배당을 해야 주주 단계 과세를 떠올리게 됩니다. 그래서 이 부분이 처음에는 낯설게 느껴집니다. 하지만 과세이연 방지 규정은 그 시점을 앞당길 수 있습니다.

고객: 그럼 세법이 실제로 현금이 안 지급됐다는 사실을 무시할 수도 있다는 건가요?

회계사: 어느 정도는 그렇습니다. 세법이 이렇게 말할 수 있는 것입니다. “돈이 외국법인 안에 남아 있는 것은 알지만, 지배구조와 저세율 환경을 고려하면 그 소득의 일부는 지금 주주에게 과세하겠다.”

고객: 그럼 실제로 돈을 받았다는 뜻이 아니라, 세법이 과세 시점을 앞당긴다는 뜻이군요.

회계사: 정확합니다. 그래서 deemed dividend라는 표현이 중요합니다. 실제 배당은 없지만, 세법상으로는 배당된 것으로 보고 과세 결과를 만드는 것입니다.

8 Korean Example: CFC Rule

Scene 3

Accountant: Let us use a Korean example. Suppose a Korean resident owns 100% of a company in Country A. Assume Country A taxes corporate income at a very low effective rate, and the Korean ownership and low-tax conditions fall within the Korean CFC rule.

Accountant: Now suppose the foreign company has the following annual results:

Item Amount Meaning
Sales KRW 3.0 billion Total revenue
Expenses KRW 2.0 billion Operating costs
Pretax income KRW 1.0 billion Profit before foreign tax
Local income tax KRW 50 million Tax paid in Country A
Retained after-tax income KRW 950 million Undistributed earnings left inside the company

Client: If there is no actual dividend, my first instinct is that there should be no Korean tax yet.

Accountant: That is the natural reaction. But if the Korean CFC rule applies, Korea may treat part of that KRW 950 million as if it had already been distributed to the Korean shareholder. That is the essence of a deemed dividend.

Client: So even though the money remains abroad, the shareholder may still have current Korean tax exposure?

Accountant: Yes. That is exactly why the rule exists. It prevents a taxpayer from saying, “I will leave the earnings offshore forever, so I should never be taxed in Korea until I choose to distribute them.”

Korean Translation

회계사: 한국 사례로 보겠습니다. 한국 거주자가 A국에 있는 회사의 지분 100%를 보유하고 있다고 가정해 보겠습니다. 그리고 A국의 실효세율이 매우 낮아서, 한국 CFC 규정의 저세율 요건과 지분 요건에 해당한다고 가정하겠습니다.

회계사: 그 외국회사의 연간 실적이 다음과 같다고 보겠습니다.

항목 금액 의미
매출 30억 원 총수익
비용 20억 원 운영비용
세전이익 10억 원 외국세 차감 전 이익
현지 법인세 5천만 원 A국에서 납부한 세금
세후 유보소득 9억 5천만 원 회사 안에 남아 있는 미배당 이익

고객: 실제 배당이 없으면 아직 한국 세금도 없다고 생각하게 됩니다.

회계사: 그 반응이 자연스럽습니다. 하지만 한국 CFC 규정이 적용되면, 한국은 그 9억 5천만 원의 일부를 한국 주주에게 이미 배당된 것처럼 볼 수 있습니다. 그것이 바로 간주배당의 핵심입니다.

고객: 그럼 돈이 해외에 그대로 있어도 주주 단계에서 현재 한국 과세가 생길 수 있다는 말이군요?

회계사: 맞습니다. 바로 그 점 때문에 이 규정이 존재합니다. “이익을 계속 해외에 쌓아 둘 테니, 내가 배당을 선택할 때까지는 한국 과세가 없어야 한다”라는 주장을 막는 것입니다.

9 U.S. Example: Subpart F

Scene 4

Client: Then what is the basic idea of Subpart F in the United States?

Accountant: Subpart F mainly targets certain categories of income earned by a controlled foreign corporation, especially income often described as passive or mobile. Examples include interest, royalties, dividends, and other kinds of income that may be easier to locate in a low-tax jurisdiction.

Accountant: Imagine a U.S. shareholder owns a Cayman company. The Cayman company earns the following income during the year:

Type of income Amount
Interest income KRW 300 million
Royalty income KRW 200 million
Dividend income KRW 100 million
Total KRW 600 million

Client: And if the Cayman company does not pay a dividend, some of that income may still be taxed in the United States?

Accountant: Yes. That is the basic policy idea of Subpart F. The United States does not want certain movable foreign income to sit offshore forever without current taxation.

Korean Translation

고객: 그럼 미국의 Subpart F는 기본적으로 어떤 개념인가요?

회계사: Subpart F는 외국지배법인이 벌어들인 특정 유형의 소득, 특히 수동적이거나 이동성이 큰 소득을 주로 겨냥합니다. 예를 들어 이자, 로열티, 배당 같은 소득은 저세율 국가에 배치하기가 비교적 쉬운 소득으로 설명되는 경우가 많습니다.

회계사: 예를 들어 미국 주주가 케이맨 회사를 하나 가지고 있고, 그 회사가 한 해 동안 다음과 같은 소득을 벌었다고 해 보겠습니다.

소득 유형 금액
이자소득 3억 원
로열티소득 2억 원
배당소득 1억 원
합계 6억 원

고객: 그리고 케이맨 회사가 배당을 하지 않아도 그 소득의 일부가 미국에서 과세될 수 있다는 말이군요?

회계사: 맞습니다. 그것이 Subpart F의 기본 정책입니다. 미국은 이동이 쉬운 특정 외국소득이 계속 해외에 쌓여 당기 과세를 피하는 것을 원하지 않습니다.

10 U.S. Example: GILTI

Scene 5

Client: Then how is GILTI different from Subpart F?

Accountant: Subpart F is often explained as focusing on certain categories of income. GILTI is broader. It was designed to bring certain low-taxed foreign earnings into current U.S. taxation even when those earnings do not fit neatly into classic Subpart F categories.

Accountant: Suppose a U.S. parent company owns an Irish subsidiary with the following annual results:

Item Amount
Sales KRW 100.0 billion
Expenses KRW 70.0 billion
Pretax income KRW 30.0 billion
Irish income tax KRW 3.75 billion
After-tax profit KRW 26.25 billion

Client: So even if the profit remains in Ireland, the U.S. parent may still have a current tax issue?

Accountant: Yes. That is where GILTI comes in. The U.S. parent may have to include a GILTI amount in current taxable income even though no actual dividend is paid.

Korean Translation

고객: 그럼 GILTI는 Subpart F와 어떻게 다른가요?

회계사: Subpart F는 주로 특정 소득 유형에 초점을 맞추는 규정으로 설명되는 경우가 많습니다. 반면 GILTI는 더 넓습니다. 전통적인 Subpart F 범주에 정확히 들어맞지 않더라도, 저세율 해외이익을 미국의 현재 과세 범위로 끌어오기 위해 만들어졌습니다.

회계사: 예를 들어 미국 모회사가 아일랜드 자회사를 보유하고 있고, 그 자회사의 연간 실적이 다음과 같다고 해 보겠습니다.

항목 금액
매출 1,000억 원
비용 700억 원
세전이익 300억 원
아일랜드 법인세 37.5억 원
세후이익 262.5억 원

고객: 그럼 그 이익이 아일랜드에 남아 있어도 미국 모회사 쪽에서 당기 과세 문제가 생길 수 있겠네요?

회계사: 맞습니다. 바로 그 부분에서 GILTI가 등장합니다. 실제 배당이 없어도 미국 모회사는 GILTI 금액을 현재 과세소득에 포함해야 할 수 있습니다.

11 Simplified U.S. Corporate Tax Adjustment Example

Scene 6

Client: Can you show me a simplified tax adjustment process as well?

Accountant: Of course. Assume the Irish subsidiary has pretax income of KRW 30.0 billion. For illustration only, suppose KRW 8.0 billion is treated as a routine return amount, and the remaining KRW 22.0 billion becomes relevant for GILTI purposes.

Step Description Amount
1 CFC tested income KRW 30.0 billion
2 Less: routine return / QBAI amount (KRW 8.0 billion)
3 GILTI inclusion amount KRW 22.0 billion
4 Section 250 deduction (illustrative assumption: 50%) (KRW 11.0 billion)
5 Net U.S. taxable amount from GILTI KRW 11.0 billion

Client: So the company does not simply pay U.S. tax on the full foreign amount without any further analysis?

Accountant: Correct. In practice, a U.S. corporation may need to consider the GILTI inclusion, possible deductions, and foreign tax credit consequences.

Accountant: For example, if the Irish subsidiary paid KRW 3.75 billion of local tax, part of that foreign tax may help reduce the U.S. tax burden, subject to applicable U.S. rules and limitations.

Korean Translation

고객: 그럼 단순화한 세무조정 흐름도 보여주실 수 있나요?

회계사: 물론입니다. 아일랜드 자회사의 세전이익이 300억 원이라고 가정하겠습니다. 설명 목적상 그중 80억 원은 정상수익으로 보고, 나머지 220억 원이 GILTI 계산과 관련된다고 단순화해 보겠습니다.

단계 설명 금액
1 CFC tested income 300억 원
2 차감: 정상수익 / QBAI 금액 (80억 원)
3 GILTI 산입금액 220억 원
4 Section 250 공제(예시상 50%) (110억 원)
5 미국 과세대상 순금액 110억 원

고객: 그럼 외국금액 전체에 대해 아무 조정 없이 그대로 미국 세금을 내는 것은 아니군요?

회계사: 그렇습니다. 실무에서는 GILTI 산입, 가능한 공제, 그리고 외국납부세액공제를 함께 검토해야 할 수 있습니다.

회계사: 예를 들어 아일랜드 자회사가 현지에서 37.5억 원의 세금을 냈다면, 그 외국세액의 일부가 미국 세부담을 줄이는 데 활용될 수 있습니다. 물론 미국 세법상 한도와 규정은 따로 검토해야 합니다.
