Audit Case Study — Consignment Sales Between Parent and Subsidiary

In this audit case, a subsidiary sells goods to customers under a consignment arrangement. The parent company retains control of the inventory. However, the subsidiary records sales, accounts receivable from customers, and accounts payable to the parent company.

Scene 1 — Understanding the Business Model

Auditor: We understand that the subsidiary distributes products that are manufactured by the parent company. Could you explain how the inventory ownership works in this arrangement? We want to confirm whether the subsidiary ever takes legal title to the goods.

Finance Manager: The parent company technically retains ownership of the inventory. Our role is mainly to sell the products to local customers. However, we manage the sales process and customer relationships independently.

Korean Translation

감사인: 자회사가 모회사의 제품을 판매하는 구조라고 이해하고 있습니다. 이 거래에서 재고의 소유권이 어떻게 되는지 설명해 주시겠습니까? 자회사가 상품의 법적 소유권을 가지는지 확인하고 싶습니다.

재무팀장: 재고의 법적 소유권은 모회사에 있습니다. 저희 역할은 주로 현지 고객에게 제품을 판매하는 것입니다. 다만 판매 과정과 고객 관리는 저희가 담당하고 있습니다.

Scene 2 — Revenue Recognition

Auditor: We noticed that the subsidiary records sales revenue when products are sold to customers. However, if the parent company retains control of the goods, the subsidiary may be acting as an agent rather than a principal. In that case, recognizing full sales revenue might not be appropriate.

Finance Manager: I see your point, but from our perspective we are the entity interacting with the customer. We issue the invoices and collect the payments. Therefore, it seems reasonable for us to record the revenue.

Korean Translation

감사인: 자회사가 고객에게 판매할 때 매출을 인식하고 있는 것을 확인했습니다. 하지만 상품의 통제권이 모회사에 있다면 자회사는 principal이 아니라 agent일 수 있습니다. 그렇다면 전체 매출을 인식하는 것은 적절하지 않을 수 있습니다.

재무팀장: 그 부분은 이해합니다. 하지만 고객과 직접 거래하는 주체는 저희입니다. 인보이스도 저희가 발행하고 대금도 저희가 수금합니다. 그래서 매출을 인식하는 것이 합리적이라고 생각했습니다.

Scene 3 — Accounts Receivable Issue

Auditor: We also observed that the subsidiary records accounts receivable from the customer. If the subsidiary is acting only as a selling agent, the receivable may legally belong to the parent company. In that case, the subsidiary may not have a valid claim against the customer.

Finance Manager: But the invoices are issued under the subsidiary's name, and customers pay us directly. Operationally, we are responsible for collecting the cash. That is why we recorded the receivable.

Korean Translation

감사인: 또한 자회사가 고객에 대한 매출채권을 인식하고 있는 것을 확인했습니다. 자회사가 단순 판매 대리인이라면 채권의 법적 권리는 모회사에 있을 가능성이 있습니다. 그렇다면 자회사가 채권을 인식하는 것은 적절하지 않을 수 있습니다.

재무팀장: 하지만 인보이스는 자회사 명의로 발행되고 있습니다. 그리고 고객들도 저희에게 직접 대금을 지급합니다. 그래서 실무적으로는 저희가 채권을 보유한다고 생각했습니다.

Scene 4 — Accounts Payable to Parent

Auditor: We also noticed that the subsidiary records accounts payable to the parent company for the full sales amount. However, if the parent company still owns the inventory, the transaction may not represent a purchase by the subsidiary. Instead, the subsidiary may only be entitled to a commission.

Finance Manager: Our accounting team believed that recognizing a payable would reflect the obligation to transfer the sales proceeds to the parent company. We thought this was the simplest way to record the transaction.

Korean Translation

감사인: 또 하나 확인한 것은 자회사가 매출 금액 전체에 대해 모회사에 대한 매입채무를 인식하고 있다는 점입니다. 하지만 재고의 소유권이 모회사에 있다면 자회사가 상품을 구매한 것이 아닐 수 있습니다. 이 경우 자회사는 단지 수수료만 받을 권리가 있을 가능성이 있습니다.

재무팀장: 저희 회계팀은 판매 대금을 모회사에 전달해야 하는 의무를 반영하기 위해 매입채무를 인식했습니다. 거래를 기록하는 가장 간단한 방법이라고 생각했습니다.

Scene 5 — Auditor Explanation

Auditor: Under accounting standards, when an entity acts as an agent, it should recognize only the commission revenue. The underlying sales transaction belongs to the principal, which in this case appears to be the parent company. Therefore, the subsidiary should not recognize the full sales amount.

Finance Manager: If that interpretation is correct, it would significantly reduce our reported revenue. Our management may need to reconsider the accounting policy.

Korean Translation

감사인: 회계 기준에 따르면 어떤 기업이 agent로 행동하는 경우 수수료 수익만 인식해야 합니다. 실제 판매 거래는 principal인 모회사에 귀속됩니다. 따라서 자회사가 전체 매출을 인식하는 것은 적절하지 않을 수 있습니다.

재무팀장: 그 해석이 맞다면 저희 매출 규모가 상당히 줄어들게 됩니다. 경영진이 회계 정책을 다시 검토해야 할 것 같습니다.

Scene 6 — Consolidation Discussion

Auditor: From a group perspective, this issue also affects the consolidated financial statements. If the subsidiary recognizes full sales and accounts payable to the parent, internal balances will need to be eliminated during consolidation. The correct presentation would show revenue only from transactions with external customers.

Finance Manager: So even if the subsidiary records the transactions this way, the consolidation process would remove the internal payable and receivable. The group financial statements would ultimately show the sales generated from customers. I understand why the classification between principal and agent is important.

Korean Translation

감사인: 그룹 관점에서는 연결재무제표에도 영향이 있습니다. 자회사가 전체 매출을 인식하고 모회사에 대한 채무를 기록하면 연결 과정에서 내부 거래를 제거해야 합니다. 연결 재무제표에서는 외부 고객과의 거래만 매출로 표시되어야 합니다.

재무팀장: 그러면 자회사 장부에 이런 거래가 기록되어 있더라도 연결 과정에서 내부 채권채무는 제거되겠군요. 결국 그룹 재무제표에는 외부 고객과의 매출만 남게 됩니다. principal과 agent 구분이 왜 중요한지 이해했습니다.

Final Consolidated Financial Statements

When the subsidiary correctly records the transaction as an agent and recognizes only commission revenue, the consolidated financial statements properly reflect the economic substance of the transaction.

  • Revenue: 100 (Sale to external customer recognized by the parent)
  • Cost of Goods Sold: 80 (Cost incurred by the parent to produce the goods)
  • Gross Profit: 20
  • Operating Expense: 0 (Internal commission of 20 is eliminated)
  • Operating Income: 20

Therefore, the consolidated profit reflects the actual margin earned from the external customer transaction.

Risk if the Subsidiary Records Gross Revenue

Under US GAAP (ASC 606), if an entity acts as an agent but records revenue on a gross basis, this may be considered revenue overstatement.

  • Audit Risk
    External auditors may require a significant audit adjustment if the subsidiary recognizes revenue of 100 even though it does not control the inventory.

  • Consolidation Complexity
    If the subsidiary records Sales 100 and Cost of Sales 80, both the parent and subsidiary would report revenue of 100. During consolidation, this would create duplicated revenue and require complicated elimination entries.

Based on ASC 606, because the subsidiary does not control the inventory, does not bear inventory risk, and does not have pricing authority, it should be classified as an agent. Therefore, the subsidiary should recognize only commission revenue.

Additional Audit Discussion (Hidden)

Possible Debate Points in the Case (Click to Expand)

The case above simplifies several accounting issues for learning purposes. However, in real audits, some points could be debated depending on contract details and accounting policies.

  • Who owns the Accounts Receivable?
    Even if the subsidiary is an agent, it may still record the receivable from the customer if it collects the cash on behalf of the parent company. Therefore, the receivable does not automatically belong to the parent.
  • Operating Expense in Consolidation
    The example assumes that internal commission expense is eliminated during consolidation. However, the subsidiary may still have its own selling expenses such as salaries, marketing costs, or logistics costs.
  • Agent vs Principal Judgement
    Under ASC 606, factors such as inventory risk, pricing discretion, and control of goods are indicators rather than strict rules. The conclusion depends on the overall assessment of the contract.

These issues are often discussed between auditors and management during revenue recognition reviews.
